# Reference — Audience Building Content Creator

This file expands on `SKILL.md`. It contains the full methodology, the Six Elements with do/don't patterns, format templates with length specs, platform notes, failure modes, short-vs-long content strategy, and worked examples.

Source methodology: `content-generation-rules/如何在没有任何粉丝的情况下建立自己的受众群.md`. When in doubt, consult the source directly.

---

## 1. Core Stance

Cold-start growth is not bottlenecked by content volume; it is bottlenecked by being seen. Every choice in this skill follows from that.

Good output should help the user:

- Become visible to a **specific** group of people.
- Express a **recognizable** point of view.
- Solve a **real** problem or explain a real tension.
- Build trust through experience, expertise, judgment, or clarity.
- Create something strangers can share, discuss, save, or follow over time.

Avoid:

- Self-impressive "art pieces" that are hard to understand or share.
- Generic advice anyone could have written.
- Trend-chasing without a durable point of view.
- Content that only expresses the creator's mood and gives the audience nothing.
- Empty networking, templated outreach, or "想跟你 virtual coffee 一下" energy in posts.

## 2. The Six Elements (核心框架)

Every output must contain all six, adapted to format. Missing any one is a failure mode.

### 2.1 Hook

The first line / first 1–3 seconds / first image must stop the right person. Hook quality includes word choice, layout, and visual organization.

**Strong hook patterns:**

- **A painful truth:** 「你不是缺内容灵感，你是缺一个别人愿意关注你的理由。」
- **A contrarian stance:** 「新账号最不该先追热点。」
- **A specific audience callout:** 「如果你是想做独立产品的工程师，这一条比 90% 的'增长建议'都重要。」
- **A concrete promise:** 「用一个框架，把零散经验变成能积累受众的内容。」
- **A familiar failure:** 「每天发内容却没人关注，通常不是因为你不够努力。」
- **Concrete claim with stakes:** 「我做了 3 年内容，前 2 年只有 80 个粉丝。我后来发现我搞错了一件事。」
- **Reframe:** 「你以为更新频率决定增长，其实分发能力才决定。」

**Avoid:**

- Broad motivational openings ("今天想跟大家聊聊…").
- Clever but unclear wording.
- Hooks that attract the wrong audience.
- Exaggerated promises the content cannot support.
- Weather-style or "abstract abstract" openers.

### 2.2 Big Problem

Name a problem the audience instantly recognizes as their own. The sharper the audience, the sharper the problem can be.

**Good problem statements:**

- Are stated in the audience's own words.
- Make the reader/viewer think "this is literally me".
- Have stakes (time wasted, money lost, status risk, missed opportunity).

**Common audience-building problems to draw on:**

- The creator talks to everyone, so no one feels addressed.
- The content shares conclusions without lived experience.
- The advice is correct but interchangeable.
- The content is polished but not useful, debatable, or memorable.
- The creator chases trends instead of building a recognizable worldview.

**Bad problem statements:**

- Abstract ("内容创作很难").
- Universal ("每个人都想成长").
- Creator-side framing ("我想分享一下我的经验").

### 2.3 Clear Solution

Give an executable path, **filtered through the user's real experience**. Generic-correct-but-useless advice ("多输出、多坚持") fails this element.

**Strong solutions:**

- Come from the user's experience or professional ability.
- Explain what to do **and** what to stop doing.
- Show the thinking behind the recommendation.
- Carry the user's fingerprint (their own constraint, mistake, or twist).
- Can be tested in the next post / article / script / conversation.

**Weak solutions:**

- 「坚持输出」「提供价值」「打造人设」「多互动」
- Any advice that sounds true but gives no next action.

### 2.4 Big Benefit

Make the payoff explicit. If the audience has to guess, they won't share or save.

Benefits can be:

- **Practical:** 「你会更快知道哪类内容值得持续做。」
- **Emotional:** 「你不需要每天怀疑自己是不是不适合做内容。」
- **Social:** 「陌生人会更容易把你归类为某个问题的可靠解决者。」
- **Identity:** 「你会从发散表达，变成一个有清晰立场的创作者。」
- **Outcome-implied:** 「我用这个结构改了 10 条帖子，平均互动翻了 3 倍。」

### 2.5 Confidence or Polarization

Take a clear position. **Polarization here does not mean trolling or rage-bait.** It means: do not write things everyone agrees with and no one remembers.

**Useful stance patterns:**

- 「真正的问题不是 X，而是 Y。」
- 「很多人以为要先做 A，其实更该先做 B。」
- 「这件事不适合所有人，但适合 [specific audience]。」
- 「我不建议新手这样做，因为…」
- A specific "do this, not that".
- A named opponent idea ("不要再追热点了，你要追的是同一类问题").

**Markers of fence-sitting (fail):**

- "Both sides have a point."
- "It depends."
- "Everyone's journey is different."
- Over-qualifying until no point remains.

### 2.6 Novel Perspective / Big Idea

Add the insight that makes the audience think "I never saw it that way."

**Sources of fresh perspective:**

- A personal failure or case.
- A professional analogy.
- A counterintuitive distinction.
- A named framework.
- A cross-domain analogy.
- A new unit of analysis ("不要看单条内容表现，看一个月的复利曲线").
- A synthesis of another creator's idea with the user's own experience.

## 3. Required Reframe (apply before writing)

Force the raw topic into this template before generating:

```text
For [specific audience],
who struggle with / care about [specific problem or desire],
this content helps them [specific benefit],
by showing [experience, insight, method, story, or point of view],
so they have a reason to [read, save, share, comment, follow, or trust].
```

**Topic narrowing examples (move from "too broad" to "specific enough to win"):**

| Too broad | Specific enough |
|---|---|
| 个人成长 | 刚开始做自由职业、没有作品集的人，如何用 30 天建立第一批信任资产 |
| AI 工具 | 内容创作者如何用 AI 把零散经验变成稳定发布的文章和脚本 |
| 职场效率 | 工作 3–5 年的专业人士，如何把忙碌转成可见的专业影响力 |
| 健身 | 久坐程序员如何在不去健身房的情况下，用 12 周改善腰背疼 |
| 写作 | 第一次写公众号、没有读者的专业人士，如何让前 10 篇就被同行转发 |

If you cannot fill the template in one sentence, ask **one targeted clarifying question** instead of producing generic content.

## 4. Self-Checks

Run **all three** before delivering. Order: Reframe → Compressed Test → Share-Check → Quality Checklist.

### 4.1 Compressed Test (one-line gut check)

The piece must give a reason to **stop · care · trust · remember · share**. If any is missing, revise.

### 4.2 Share-Check (3 questions)

1. **Why would anyone share this?** (status, identity, usefulness, "this is exactly what I think", surprise)
2. **Does the target audience recognize themselves in it?** (concrete enough, named situations, named pains)
3. **Is the position sharp enough to provoke discussion, saves, or quotes?**

Sharing is an act of self-expression for the audience. Content must give them something worth expressing.

### 4.3 Share-Trigger Inventory

A strong piece usually gives at least one of:

- **Identity expression:** "这说出了我正在经历的事。"
- **Utility:** "这能帮到我身边某类人。"
- **Judgment:** "这个观点替我表达了一个判断。"
- **Novelty:** "这个解释很新，我想保存或转发。"
- **Discussion:** "这个立场值得评论或反驳。"
- **Trust:** "这个人不像在套模板，他真的懂这个问题。"

If none apply, revise the hook, problem, benefit, or point of view.

## 5. Format Templates

Use as defaults. Adapt to platform conventions, the user's voice, and any length specified by the user.

### 5.1 Article Outline

```markdown
## 定位
- 目标读者：[specific audience]
- 内容承诺：[what the reader gets]
- 核心角度：[user's spine — experience / opinion / capability]

## 标题候选
1.
2.
3.

## 大纲
1. Hook / 开场判断
2. 具体问题（用读者的语言）
3. 个人经验或专业观察（spine）
4. 核心方法或观点（含 novel perspective）
5. 例子 / 步骤 / 案例
6. 对读者的收益（big benefit, restated）
7. 结尾与行动建议（CTA / share trigger）

## 受众积累逻辑
[Why this helps the right people trust, remember, share, or follow]
```

### 5.2 Full Article

Recommended flow:

1. **Hook** (1–3 sentences).
2. **Big Problem** in the audience's words (1 short paragraph).
3. **Position / thesis** (1–2 sentences, clearly stated).
4. **3–5 sections**, each carrying one argument with one piece of evidence or one example from the user's experience.
5. **Novel perspective made explicit** somewhere in the middle — the reframe.
6. **Conclusion:** takeaway restated, benefit restated, soft invitation to act or share.

**Length defaults (adjust to user's platform):**

- 公众号 / Substack / Medium: 1200–2500 字 for a single idea.
- LinkedIn long post: 600–1200 字.
- 知乎回答 / 长贴: depends on the question.

### 5.3 Short-Form Video Script (15–60s)

```markdown
## 定位
- 目标观众：[specific]
- 视频承诺：[what they walk away with]
- 观点：[the one-line stance]

## 脚本
Hook (0–3s)
  Spoken: <stop-scroll line>
  On-screen text: <optional>
Problem (3–8s)
  Spoken: <named problem in audience's language>
Solution beats (8–45s)
  Beat 1: <one concrete step / point + example>
  Beat 2: ...
  Beat 3: ...
Benefit + Takeaway (45–60s)
  Spoken: <what they got, what to do next>
  On-screen text: <key phrase to save>

## 可替换 Hook
1.
2.
3.

## Share trigger
[Why someone would send this to a friend]
```

**Do not** include filming, editing, equipment, lighting, transitions, music, or post-production notes.

### 5.4 Long-Form Video Voiceover / 口播稿

Format rules:

- Write in **spoken rhythm**: short sentences, natural breath points.
- Mark structural beats: `【开场 Hook】`, `【问题】`, `【核心观点】`, `【解决方案 1/2/3】`, `【新视角】`, `【收尾 + 行动】`.
- Use line breaks generously — one idea per line where possible.
- Mark emphases sparingly (e.g. `**重点**`) only where the user should stress on camera.
- **Length estimation:** ~250–300 字/分钟 (中文) or ~150 words/min (English). Compute back from the user's target duration.

### 5.5 Social Media Image-Text Post (小红书, LinkedIn, 公众号短贴, X thread, Threads)

Structure:

1. **Title / first line** — hook + audience signal.
2. **Setup** — name the problem in 1–2 lines.
3. **Body** — 3–5 numbered or bulleted beats, each one concrete.
4. **Takeaway** — one line that crystallizes the position.
5. **Share trigger** — one line that gives a reason to save or send.

For carousel / 图文 posts, add a page-by-page structure:

```markdown
封面: [hook + 受众识别词]
第 1 页: [problem]
第 2 页: [mistake / contrast]
第 3 页: [solution beat 1]
第 4 页: [solution beat 2]
第 5 页: [example / case]
结尾页 / CTA: [takeaway + 分享/收藏理由]
配文: [发布时的正文文案]
```

**Platform notes:**

- **小红书:** punchy title, concrete scenarios, save-worthy structure, emojis sparingly only if user's voice supports it.
- **LinkedIn:** opinion-led opener, story or example, structured body, takeaway, single question to drive comments.
- **X / Twitter thread:** tweet 1 = hook + promise; tweets 2..n-1 = one beat each; final tweet = takeaway + soft CTA. Each tweet must stand alone enough to be quoted.
- **公众号 / newsletter:** deeper argument, trust-building story, frameworks, long-term relationship.
- **Threads / 微博:** sharp single thought, quotable, lower formality.

### 5.6 Content Topic List

Default count: **8**. Range: **5–15** depending on request size. For each topic:

```markdown
- Title (working): <sharp working title>
  For whom: <specific audience>
  Big problem: <one line>
  Novel angle: <the reframe / fresh take>
  Big benefit: <one line>
  Best format: <article / short video / voiceover / image-text / thread>
  Why it attracts the right people: <one line>
```

Bias the list toward topics that:

- Stay within **one consistent theme** (so the audience knows what you're for).
- Come from the user's real experience or expertise.
- Can each pass the share-check on their own.

Avoid lists of titles only. Titles without audience logic do not help long-term growth.

### 5.7 Content Optimization Suggestions

When the user provides existing content to improve:

1. **Diagnose against the Six Elements** — name which are present, which are weak, which are missing.
2. **Run the Share-Check** on the original.
3. **Give specific rewrites, not vague advice.** At minimum:
   - Rewrite the hook (2 alternatives).
   - Sharpen the problem statement.
   - Tighten the position to remove fence-sitting.
   - Suggest the novel-perspective angle the piece could land on.
4. End with **1–3 concrete actions** the user can apply across future pieces, not just this one.

## 6. Failure Modes (What to Avoid)

- **Self-indulgent artistry** — content that satisfies the creator's aesthetic but the market does not care about.
- **Trend-chasing without a spine** — riding a hot topic with no personal angle.
- **"Everyone agrees" platitudes** — no position, no memory, no shares.
- **Generic explainers** — anyone could have written it; nothing of the user in it.
- **Template-style outreach voice** — "想跟你 virtual coffee 一下" energy in posts.
- **Comment-section spam logic** — repeating the original post, or replying just to be seen.
- **Audience set to "everyone"** — guaranteed to land with no one.
- **Hook that attracts the wrong audience** — high views, zero conversion to followers.
- **Solution = correct-but-useless** ("坚持输出" / "提供价值").
- **For video creation requests: drifting into filming / editing / equipment advice.** This skill stays on the content side only.

---

## 7. Long-Term Brand Bias

Prioritize stable audience accumulation over short-term traffic tricks.

**Prefer:**

- Repeatable themes that build recognition.
- Personal experience plus useful abstraction.
- Content that can become a series.
- Short posts that can lead into longer articles, newsletters, podcasts, or videos.
- Clear positioning around a problem the creator can keep serving.
- Collaboration, quoting, commentary, and thoughtful replies that create real connection.

**Avoid:**

- Trend-chasing unrelated to the user's long-term direction.
- Generic "viral" formulas with no audience fit.
- Self-indulgent storytelling without reader benefit.
- Over-polished content that takes too long to validate.
- Empty replies, template networking, or fake intimacy.
- Content that only performs by provoking but does not build trust.

---

## 8. Short vs. Long Content Strategy

When the user is planning a body of work rather than a single piece, remind them (briefly) of the structural logic from the source:

- **Short content** (posts, tweets, reels) is the **entry funnel** — it earns the first 10 seconds of attention.
- **Long content** (articles, newsletters, podcasts, long videos) **occupies 15–60 minutes** of attention and builds deep trust and topic ownership.
- A healthy plan **converts the best short pieces into long-form assets**. Every short post that performs is a candidate seed for a longer piece.

Use this only when planning, not in every single output.

## 9. Worked Examples

### 9.1 小红书短帖

**User request:** 「我是一个做了 4 年后端的工程师，想转独立开发者，帮我写一条小红书。」

**Required Reframe:**
- For: 3–5 年经验、想做独立产品的后端工程师
- Problem: 以为卡点是技术，疯狂补前端/AI/设计
- Benefit: 少走前 6 个月的弯路
- Spine: 用户自己从打工到独立开发的真实卡点
- Novel angle: 「你卡的不是技术，是选题和分发」
- Share trigger: 同类工程师转发表达"这就是我"

**Output:**

```text
后端工程师转独立开发，前 6 个月我做错的一件事

我做了 4 年后端，去年决定转独立开发。
前 6 个月，我以为技术不够，疯狂补前端、补 AI、补设计。
后来我发现：卡住我的根本不是技术。

是选题，和没人知道我在做什么。

3 件我后来才想明白的事：
1. 你不缺工具，你缺一个值得做半年的具体问题。
2. 第一批用户不会从应用商店来，他们会从你公开写过的东西来。
3. 工程师做独立产品，最大的杠杆不是再学一门技术，是开始在公开场合写你解决的问题。

如果你也是工程师在想转独立开发——
先选一个你真的懂、真的烦过的问题，
再开始公开地写它。

技术不是你最缺的那块拼图。
```

**Six Elements check:**
- Hook: 具体身份 + 反直觉判断 ✓
- Big Problem: 工程师转独立卡在哪里 ✓
- Clear Solution: 三条可执行 ✓
- Big Benefit: 少走前 6 个月的弯路 ✓
- Position: 明确"技术不是你最缺的那块" ✓
- Novel Perspective: 卡点是选题和分发，不是技术 ✓

**Compressed test:** 停（具体身份钩）· 在意（同类痛点）· 信（4 年经历）· 记（"技术不是你最缺的那块"）· 分享（"这就是我"）✓

### 9.2 文章大纲（公众号）

**User request:** 「帮我写一篇公众号文章大纲，主题是'内容创作者如何用 AI 把零散经验变成稳定输出'」

**Required Reframe:**
- For: 有专业经验但发布频率不稳定的内容创作者
- Problem: 想法很多但写不出来，每次写都从零开始
- Benefit: 用 AI 把零散经验沉淀成可复用的内容资产
- Spine: 创作者自己的真实工作流
- Novel angle: 「AI 的杠杆不在写，而在'结构化你已经懂的东西'」

**Output:**

```markdown
## 定位
- 目标读者：有专业经验但发布频率不稳定的内容创作者（公众号、newsletter、知识博主）
- 内容承诺：一个能让你每周稳定输出 1–2 篇高质量内容的 AI 协作流程
- 核心角度：AI 不是替你写，而是替你把"已经懂的东西"结构化

## 标题候选
1. 内容创作者用 AI，最大的误区是让它替你写
2. 我用 AI 写了 30 篇文章，最有用的不是它生成的字
3. AI 不会让你写得更快，它让你写得更稳

## 大纲
1. Hook：很多人用 AI 是从"写一篇关于 X 的文章"开始的，所以越用越没灵魂
2. 问题：创作者真正的卡点不是"不会写"，是"想法没结构、经验没整理"
3. Spine：我自己用 AI 一年的工作流变化
4. 核心观点：AI 的杠杆 = 把你已经懂的东西，问出来、结构化、变成系列
5. 方法（3 步）：
   - 用 AI 做"采访式"提问，把你脑中的隐性知识倒出来
   - 用 AI 做结构化（论点 / 证据 / 案例 / 反例）
   - 用 AI 做"系列化"——把一篇拆成 3–5 个角度
6. 例子：我把一次客户复盘，用这套流程拆成了 4 篇文章
7. 收益：你不再为"今天发什么"焦虑，而是为"这个系列怎么深入"投入
8. 结尾 + CTA：评论留下你最想结构化的一个专业经验，我会挑 3 个用这个流程示范

## 受众积累逻辑
专业人士看到这篇会想"我也有一堆没整理的经验"，
分享时表达的是身份（"我是认真做内容的人，不是套模板的人"），
而不是工具技巧。
```

### 9.3 60 秒口播稿

**User request:** 「我做内容策略咨询的，帮我写一条 60 秒的口播，讲'为什么大多数公司的内容账号做不起来'」

**Required Reframe:**
- For: 负责公司内容账号但增长卡住的市场/品牌负责人
- Problem: 发了一年内容没增长，老板开始质疑
- Benefit: 知道真正要换的是定位，不是更新频率
- Spine: 咨询师见过的真实公司案例
- Novel angle: 「公司账号做不起来，是因为它在替'公司'说话，不是替'某一类人'说话」

**Output (~250 字, ~60 秒):**

```text
【开场 Hook】（0–5s）
大多数公司的内容账号做不起来，
不是因为发得少。

【问题】（5–15s）
我做内容策略咨询 5 年，
见过太多公司每天发 3 条，做了一年还在 2000 粉丝。
他们都在问同一个问题：是不是要换平台？

【核心观点】（15–30s）
都不是。
真正的问题是——
你的账号在替"公司"说话，
不是替"某一类具体的人"说话。

【新视角 + 解决方案】（30–50s）
个人能涨粉，是因为一个人天然就有具体身份。
公司想涨粉，得先**主动选择**一个具体的人。
不是"我们服务所有中小企业"，
是"我们替那个'第一次招 AI 工程师、不知道怎么面试'的 CTO 说话"。
你的内容只要让那一类人觉得"这是替我做的"，
他会自己开始转发、自己开始关注、自己开始信任你。

【收尾】（50–60s）
所以公司账号做不起来，
**换的不是频率，是替谁说话**。
```

**Six Elements check:**
- Hook: 反直觉 + 行业判断 ✓
- Big Problem: 一年 2000 粉，老板开始质疑 ✓
- Clear Solution: 选一个具体身份的人去服务 ✓
- Big Benefit: 不再换平台试错 ✓
- Position: 「换的不是频率，是替谁说话」✓
- Novel Perspective: 公司账号要"主动选择身份" ✓

## 10. Audience Lock-In Checklist (final guard)

Before producing any output, confirm internally:

- [ ] I can name the specific audience in one sentence.
- [ ] I can name the one big problem this piece solves for them.
- [ ] I can name the user's spine (experience, opinion, or capability) for this piece.
- [ ] I can name the share trigger.

If any of these is fuzzy, ask **one** targeted clarifying question rather than producing generic content.
